#  Superstore Sales Analysis Using SQL

##  Project Overview

This project analyzes the **Superstore Sales Dataset** using **SQL** to answer key business questions related to sales, profit, customers, products, and regional performance. The project demonstrates SQL skills including data definition, data manipulation, aggregation, filtering, window functions, and business insight generation.

---

##  Objectives

- Create and manage a retail sales database
- Perform business-oriented SQL analysis
- Identify profitable and loss-making products
- Analyze regional and customer performance
- Study the impact of discounts on profitability
- Generate actionable business insights

##  Business Questions Solved

### 1. Which product category generates the highest profit?

Identifies the most profitable product category.

### 2. Which sub-categories are the most and least profitable?

Ranks product sub-categories based on total profit.

### 3. What are the total sales and profit by region?

Compares business performance across regions.

### 4. Which state contributes the highest profit?

Finds the top-performing state.

### 5. Which sub-categories generate losses?

Identifies loss-making product segments.

### 6. Which products are consistently loss-making?

Finds products that always produce negative profit.

### 7. Which region and customer segment have the lowest profitability?

Analyzes regional and customer segment performance.

### 8. What are the top 10 loss-making orders?

Identifies orders causing the largest financial losses.

### 9. How do discounts affect profit?

Studies the relationship between discounts and profitability.

### 10. Which sub-categories have the lowest profit margins?

Calculates profit margins for each sub-category.

### 11. What are the monthly sales and profit trends?

Analyzes seasonality in sales and profit.

### 12. What is the year-over-year sales growth?

Measures business growth over time.

### 13. Who are the top 5 customers by profit?

Identifies the most valuable customers.

### 14. Rank sub-categories within each category based on profit.

Uses SQL Window Functions to rank product performance.

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- Technology products generate the highest profits.
- Some furniture-related sub-categories consistently produce losses.
- Higher discounts generally reduce profitability.
- California is one of the strongest profit-generating states.
- A small group of customers contributes a large share of profits.
- Sales and profits vary significantly across regions and months.
- Not all high-sales products are profitable.
- Window functions help compare product performance within categories.
